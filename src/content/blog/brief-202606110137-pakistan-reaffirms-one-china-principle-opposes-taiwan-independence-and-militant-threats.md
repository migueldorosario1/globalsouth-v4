<p>On Taiwan, Pakistan reaffirmed its adherence to the one-China principle, describing the democratically governed island as an “inalienable” part of China and opposing any form of independence. Pakistan also welcomed China's efforts to facilitate dialogue with Afghanistan, and both sides opposed the use of their territories by militant groups—including the Tehreek-e-Taliban Pakistan and the Eastern Turkistan Islamic Movement—to threaten regional security.</p>

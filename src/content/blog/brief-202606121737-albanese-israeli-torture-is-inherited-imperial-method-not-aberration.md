<p>Francesca Albanese, UN Special Rapporteur on the Occupied Palestinian Territories and a senior official with the Office of the High Commissioner for Human Rights, has traced systemic sexual torture against Palestinians to direct institutional and personnel continuities between British colonial counterinsurgency in Ireland (1920–1922), French doctrine in Algeria (1950s), and apartheid South Africa — all inherited and operationalised by Israel since its founding in 1948. Speaking in Al Jazeera’s documentary *Bodies of Evidence*, Albanese stressed that such violence is not aberrational but “the inheritance of an imperial method” refined across empires and embedded in Israeli law and practice.</p>
<p>Albanese noted that Britain’s emergency regulations — developed during the Irish War of Independence and deployed by former Black and Tans redeployed to Mandate Palestine in 1922 — were adopted wholesale into Israel’s legal framework after 1948. These included administrative detention, warrantless arrest, and immunity clauses for security personnel acting “in good faith”.</p>
<p>Abdel Latif Ghaith, director of the Palestinian prisoner-rights organisation Addameer, recounted witnessing the sexualised torture of Rasmea Odeh in Jerusalem in 1969 — including forced nudity, rape with objects, and coercive threats involving her father — decades before international conventions formally codified such acts as torture.</p>
<p>Kifaya Khraim, international advocacy coordinator at the Ramallah-based Women’s Centre for Legal Aid and Counselling (WCLAC), confirmed that sexual violence remains systematic, citing survivors’ accounts of invasive procedures deliberately obscured by stigma and silence. She underscored that Israeli forces exploit social taboos to evade accountability. The analysis reframes current abuses not as isolated violations but as manifestations of a transnational, racially ordered regime of control — one demanding reparations, structural accountability, and renewed Global South solidarity against enduring impunity.</p>

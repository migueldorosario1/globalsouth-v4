<p>TEL AVIV/TEHRAN — Israel launched airstrikes against military targets in western and central Iran on June 8, retaliating for a Tehran missile attack that shattered the fragile April 8 ceasefire, and ignoring a personal appeal from US President Donald Trump to hold fire. The strikes, 100 days into the conflict that began with the joint US-Israeli killing of Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, hit areas including Isfahan and Tehran, Iranian state television reported.</p>
<p>Israel’s military said the air force struck “military targets belonging to the Iranian terror regime” without elaborating. Iran’s paramilitary Revolutionary Guard confirmed that air-launched ballistic missiles were used in the assault. The exchange followed an Iranian missile barrage on Israel, itself a response to earlier Israeli strikes on Beirut’s southern suburbs, where the Lebanese Shiite militia Hezbollah is battling Israeli forces occupying southern Lebanon.</p>
<p>The flare-up threatens to reignite a full-scale regional war, with global energy supplies already under pressure. Iran’s blockade of the Strait of Hormuz has choked off roughly one-fifth of the world’s oil and natural gas transit, while Yemen’s Houthi rebels launched a missile at Israel on Monday, triggering sirens across the country. Saudi Arabia also reported a missile alert near a base hosting US forces, though the danger later passed.</p>
<p>President Trump had telephoned Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu on June 7 to urge restraint, but the Israeli leader proceeded nonetheless. Trump later said he was “not happy” about the Lebanon strikes and wants Iran to return to negotiations. Talks to restore the ceasefire remain deadlocked, leaving the region on a knife’s edge.</p>
